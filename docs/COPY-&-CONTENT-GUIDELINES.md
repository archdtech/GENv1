# Copy & Content Guidelines

## Voice & Tone

### Brand Personality
**MetaBite's voice is: Expert but Approachable, Scientific but Human, Empowering but Not Prescriptive**

We are your knowledgeable health partner who translates complex science into actionable advice. We're confident in our scientific foundation but speak with warmth and understanding. We empower users with knowledge rather than dictating rules.

### Voice Characteristics

#### Expert & Credible
- **Use:** Scientific terminology when appropriate, backed by research
- **Avoid:** Overpromising, miracle claims, or unscientific assertions
- **Example:** "Research shows that individuals with the FTO gene variant may benefit from..."
- **Not:** "This gene will make you lose weight instantly!"

#### Empathetic & Supportive
- **Use:** Understanding language that acknowledges health journeys are challenging
- **Avoid:** Judgmental language or shaming
- **Example:** "Many people find it challenging to maintain consistent eating habits. Here's how we can help..."
- **Not:** "You need to stop eating junk food and get serious about your health."

#### Empowering & Encouraging
- **Use:** Language that focuses on possibilities and user agency
- **Avoid:** Commanding language or removing user choice
- **Example:** "Based on your genetic profile, you might consider trying..."
- **Not:** "You must eat this way for optimal health."

#### Clear & Accessible
- **Use:** Simple language that explains complex concepts
- **Avoid:** Jargon without explanation, overly technical language
- **Example:** "Your DNA affects how your body processes fats. Some people metabolize fats more slowly than others."
- **Not:** "Lipid metabolism rates are modulated by genetic polymorphisms in lipid metabolism genes."

### Tone by Context

#### Educational Content
- **Tone:** Informative, precise, engaging
- **Focus:** Teaching complex concepts simply
- **Style:** More formal, but still accessible

#### User Interactions
- **Tone:** Friendly, encouraging, supportive
- **Focus:** Guiding users through actions
- **Style:** Conversational, direct

#### Error Messages
- **Tone:** Helpful, constructive, non-blaming
- **Focus:** Solving problems, not highlighting mistakes
- **Style:** Clear, solution-oriented

#### Success Messages
- **Tone:** Celebratory, motivating, genuine
- **Focus:** Acknowledging achievement
- **Style:** Enthusiastic but not over-the-top

#### Notifications
- **Tone:** Timely, relevant, respectful
- **Focus:** Providing value without being intrusive
- **Style:** Concise, action-oriented

## Writing Principles

### Clarity & Simplicity
- **Reading Level:** Aim for Grade 8-10 reading level
- **Sentence Length:** Average 15-20 words
- **Paragraph Length:** Maximum 3-4 sentences
- **Use:** Simple words and short sentences
- **Avoid:** Complex terminology unless clearly explained

### Active Voice
- **Use:** Active voice for direct, clear communication
- **Example:** "Upload your DNA file to receive personalized insights"
- **Not:** "Personalized insights can be received when your DNA file is uploaded"

### Positive Framing
- **Use:** Positive language that focuses on benefits and possibilities
- **Example:** "Choose foods that align with your genetic profile"
- **Not:** "Avoid foods that don't match your genetics"

### Specificity & Precision
- **Use:** Specific, measurable language
- **Example:** "Increase protein intake by 10-15 grams per meal"
- **Not:** "Eat more protein"

### Consistency
- **Use:** Consistent terminology across all touchpoints
- **Maintain:** Same voice and tone regardless of platform
- **Ensure:** All team members follow the same guidelines

### Cultural Sensitivity
- **Use:** Inclusive language that respects diversity
- **Avoid:** Cultural assumptions or stereotypes
- **Consider:** Global audience with different backgrounds

### Accessibility
- **Use:** Clear, descriptive language for screen readers
- **Provide:** Alternative text for all images
- **Ensure:** Color is not the only conveyer of information

### Prohibited Terms

#### Medical Claims
| Prohibited Term | Reason | Alternative |
|----------------|--------|-------------|
| **Cure/Treat** | Implies medical treatment | "Support", "May help", "Associated with" |
| **Diagnose** | Medical diagnosis requires licensed professionals | "Identify patterns", "Suggest" |
| **Prevent Disease** | Overpromise of disease prevention | "Support health", "May reduce risk" |
| **Guarantee** | Absolute claims not scientifically appropriate | "May help", "Can support" |
| **Medical Advice** | We don't provide medical advice | "Health insights", "Educational content" |

#### Exaggerated Claims
| Prohibited Term | Reason | Alternative |
|----------------|--------|-------------|
| **Miracle** | Overpromises results | "Effective", "Beneficial" |
| **Instant/Immediate** | Unrealistic timeline | "Gradual", "Over time" |
| **Effortless** | Minimizes user effort required | "Simple", "Streamlined" |
| **Revolutionary** | Overused marketing term | "Innovative", "Advanced" |
| **Secret** | Implies hidden knowledge | "Research-backed", "Science-based" |

#### Stigmatizing Language
| Prohibited Term | Reason | Alternative |
|----------------|--------|-------------|
| **Good/Bad Foods** | Moral judgment of food | "Nutrient-dense", "Occasional foods" |
| **Cheat/Cheating** | Implies moral failure | "Off-plan", "Flexible eating" |
| **Fat/Overweight** | Can be stigmatizing | Use person-first language |
| **Diet** | Implies temporary restriction | "Eating pattern", "Nutrition approach" |
| **Willpower** | Oversimplifies behavior change | "Habits", "Consistency", "Systems" |

### Accessibility (a11y) Guidelines

#### Alt Text Requirements
- **Decorative Images:** Use empty alt="" (decorative)
- **Informative Images:** Describe the content and purpose
- **Complex Images:** Provide detailed descriptions
- **Charts/Graphs:** Summarize the key takeaway
- **Icons:** Describe the action or meaning

**Examples:**
- **Good:** "DNA helix icon representing genetic analysis"
- **Bad:** "DNA picture"
- **Good:** "Bar chart showing daily protein intake vs. genetic recommendation"
- **Bad:** "Graph about protein"

#### Link Descriptions
- **Descriptive:** Make link text descriptive of destination
- **Unique:** Avoid "click here" or "read more"
- **Context:** Provide context about where the link goes
- **Action-Oriented:** Use verbs that describe the action

**Examples:**
- **Good:** "View your genetic profile"
- **Bad:** "Click here"
- **Good:** "Download your nutrient analysis report"
- **Bad:** "Download now"

#### Form Labels
- **Clear:** Every form input must have a clear label
- **Associated:** Labels must be programmatically associated with inputs
- **Instructions:** Provide clear instructions for complex inputs
- **Error Messages:** Provide specific, helpful error messages

#### ARIA Labels
- **Interactive Elements:** All interactive elements need ARIA labels
- **Landmarks:** Use proper ARIA landmarks for page structure
- **States:** Communicate state changes (expanded/collapsed, etc.)
- **Live Regions:** Use for dynamic content updates

### Data Privacy Requirements

#### GDPR Compliance
- **Transparency:** Clear explanation of data processing
- **Consent:** Explicit consent for data processing
- **Access:** Users can access their personal data
- **Portability:** Users can export their data
- **Rectification:** Users can correct inaccurate data
- **Erasure:** Users can request data deletion
- **Objection:** Users can object to processing

#### CCPA Compliance
- **Notice:** Clear privacy notice at collection
- **Opt-Out:** Ability to opt-out of data sales
- **Access:** Right to know what data is collected
- **Deletion:** Right to delete personal information
- **Non-Discrimination:** No discrimination for exercising rights

#### HIPAA Considerations
- **Protected Health Information (PHI):** Identify and protect PHI
- **Business Associate Agreements:** With healthcare partners
- **Security Measures:** Administrative, physical, technical safeguards
- **Breach Notification:** Procedures for data breach notification
- **Minimum Necessary:** Use only minimum necessary PHI

#### Mandatory Language

**Data Collection:**
```
We collect your genetic data and health information to provide personalized insights. 
This data is processed securely and used only for the purposes described in our Privacy Policy.
```

**Data Sharing:**
```
Your data will never be sold to third parties. We share data only with your explicit 
consent or as required by law. You control your data sharing preferences at all times.
```

**Data Security:**
```
Your data is protected using bank-level encryption and security measures. 
We regularly audit our security practices to ensure your information remains safe.
```

**User Rights:**
```
You have the right to access, correct, export, or delete your personal data at any time. 
Contact our privacy team to exercise these rights.
```

## Content Review Process

### Review Checklist
- [ ] Voice and tone consistent with brand guidelines
- [ ] Reading level appropriate for target audience
- [ ] All claims substantiated and compliant
- [ ] Accessibility requirements met
- [ ] Cultural sensitivity considered
- [ ] Legal disclaimers included where required
- [ ] Terminology consistent with approved glossary
- [ ] Microcopy follows established patterns
- [ ] Data privacy language included where relevant
- [ ] Scientific accuracy verified

### Approval Workflow
1. **Content Creation:** Writer creates draft following guidelines
2. **Scientific Review:** Science team verifies accuracy
3. **Legal Review:** Legal team ensures compliance
4. **UX Review:** Design team checks user experience
5. **Final Approval:** Content lead gives final approval
6. **Publication:** Content published with version tracking
7. **Monitoring:** Performance and feedback monitored
