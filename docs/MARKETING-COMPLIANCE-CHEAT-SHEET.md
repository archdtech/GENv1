## Screenshots & Video Guidelines

### Approved UI States for Marketing

#### ‚úÖ Approved States
- **Dashboard Overview:** Showing user stats, DNA insights, and quick actions
- **DNA Profile Display:** Trait cards with color-coded risk levels (no actual genetic data)
- **Meal Logging Interface:** Form with food search and nutritional breakdown
- **Activity Tracking:** Workout logging with DNA-based recommendations
- **Progress Analytics:** Charts showing trends and improvements (demo data)
- **Achievement Celebrations:** Milestone notifications and badge displays
- **Educational Content:** Article previews and learning progress
- **Success States:** Completed actions with positive feedback

#### ‚ùå Prohibited States
- **Error Messages:** Any error screens or failure states
- **Empty States:** Screens with no user data or "getting started" states
- **Loading States:** Progress bars or loading animations
- **Settings/Privacy:** Any screens showing sensitive user settings
- **Medical Data:** Any screens that could be interpreted as medical data
- **Other Users' Data:** Screens showing real user information
- **Incomplete Features:** Beta or unfinished features
- **Debug/Development:** Any development or debug interfaces

### Screenshot Annotation Requirements

#### Mandatory Annotations
- **"Demo Data"** watermark on all screenshots
- **"UI Subject to Change"** disclaimer
- **Feature version number or date
- **Platform indicator (iOS/Android/Web)

#### Annotation Placement
- **Bottom Right Corner:** "Demo Data" watermark
- **Bottom Left Corner:** Version and date
- **Top Center:** "UI Subject to Change" (if showing pre-release features)

#### Font and Styling
- **Font:** Arial or Helvetica, 10-12pt
- **Color:** Semi-transparent black or white for contrast
- **Opacity:** 60-70% opacity to be visible but not distracting

### Video Production Guidelines

#### Approved Content
- **User Journey Tutorials:** Step-by-step feature demonstrations
- **Benefit Explainers:** How features help users achieve goals
- **Success Stories:** Testimonials with actual users (with consent)
- **Expert Interviews:** Discussions with genetic counselors or health professionals
- **Animated Explainers:** Educational content about genetics and health

#### Production Standards
- **Resolution:** Minimum 1080p for all video content
- **Audio:** Clear voiceover with background music (volume balanced)
- **Branding:** Consistent logo and color usage throughout
- **Pacing:** 2-3 minutes maximum for feature videos
- **Captions:** Required for all video content
- **Transcript:** Available on video hosting platform

#### Demo Data Requirements
- **Fictional Profiles:** Use completely fictional user profiles
- **Realistic but Fake:** Data should look realistic but be clearly fabricated
- **No Real Genetic Data:** Never use actual genetic information
- **Consistent Data:** Use consistent demo data across all materials
- **Data Anonymization:** Remove any potentially identifiable information

### Data Anonymization Rules

#### Personal Information
- **Names:** Use common fictional names (Jane Doe, John Smith)
- **Emails:** Use example.com domain (jane.doe@example.com)
- **Locations:** Use generic locations (Springfield, Anytown)
- **Dates:** Use dates that are clearly in the past or future
- **Ages:** Use age ranges rather than specific ages

#### Health Data
- **Genetic Data:** Never show real genetic markers or health risks
- **Nutritional Data:** Use realistic but fabricated numbers
- **Activity Data:** Show reasonable but fictional workout data
- **Progress Data:** Demonstrate positive trends without specific claims
- **Medical Information:** Avoid any specific medical conditions or treatments

#### Visual Anonymization
- **Profile Photos:** Use stock photos or illustrations
- **Usernames:** Use generic usernames (user123, healthfan)
- **Location Data:** Blot out or generalize location information
- **Device Information:** Remove device identifiers and serial numbers
- **Timestamps:** Use generic or clearly fabricated timestamps

## Social Media & Ad Copy Checklist

### Pre-Publishing Checklist

#### Terminology Compliance
- [ ] Only use approved terms from terminology glossary
- [ ] No prohibited medical claims (cure, treat, diagnose)
- [ ] No exaggerated claims (miracle, instant, guaranteed)
- [ ] No stigmatizing language (good/bad foods, cheat days)
- [ ] All scientific claims have appropriate qualifiers

#### Claim Substantiation
- [ ] All factual claims can be supported with evidence
- [ ] Scientific claims cite reputable sources
- [ ] Feature claims accurately reflect product capabilities
- [ ] Benefit claims are realistic and achievable
- [ ] Testimonials are from real users with proper consent

#### Legal & Compliance
- [ ] Required disclaimers included where necessary
- [ ] Privacy policy link included in all ads
- [ ] Terms of service link included where relevant
- [ ] No unauthorized use of third-party trademarks
- [ ] All claims comply with FTC guidelines

#### Platform-Specific Requirements
- [ ] Facebook: Ad follows Facebook's health products policies
- [ ] Instagram: Visual content meets community guidelines
- [ ] Twitter: Character limits respected with proper disclaimers
- [ ] LinkedIn: Professional tone maintained
- [ ] TikTok: Content follows health content guidelines

## Compliance Monitoring

### Regular Review Schedule
- **Weekly:** Social media content review
- **Monthly:** Marketing materials audit
- **Quarterly:** Full compliance review
- **Annually:** Legal and regulatory update review

### Red Flags to Watch For
- **Unsubstantiated Claims:** Any claims without supporting evidence
- **Medical Language:** Terms that imply medical treatment or diagnosis
- **Guarantees:** Absolute promises of results or outcomes
- **Comparative Claims:** Claims that imply superiority over competitors
- **Testimonial Issues:** User stories that make medical claims

### Reporting Requirements
- **Violations:** Report to legal team within 24 hours
- **Customer Complaints:** Document and address promptly
- **Regulatory Changes:** Update materials within 30 days
- **Platform Policy Changes:** Review and update within 14 days

### Training Requirements
- **New Hires:** Compliance training within first week
- **Marketing Team:** Quarterly refresher training
- **Content Creators:** Monthly guideline reviews
- **Leadership:** Annual compliance overview

## Emergency Response Protocol

### Social Media Crisis Response
1. **Immediate Action:** Pause all scheduled posts
2. **Assessment:** Evaluate the nature and severity of the issue
3. **Legal Consultation:** Contact legal team immediately
4. **Response Plan:** Develop approved response within 2 hours
5. **Public Response:** Issue statement through official channels
6. **Follow-up:** Monitor situation and provide updates as needed

### Compliance Violation Response
1. **Documentation:** Record all details of the violation
2. **Containment:** Remove or correct violating content
3. **Investigation:** Determine root cause and scope
4. **Correction:** Implement preventive measures
5. **Reporting:** Report to appropriate regulatory bodies if required
6. **Review:** Update processes to prevent recurrence

### Customer Complaint Response
1. **Acknowledge:** Respond within 24 hours
2. **Investigate:** Gather all relevant information
3. **Resolve:** Address the customer's concern appropriately
4. **Document:** Record the complaint and resolution
5. **Analyze:** Look for patterns in complaints
6. **Improve:** Update processes based on findings
